How do we use cookies?


Cookies are small data files and can be stored on a computer’s hard drive. These would only be stored in case the user’s web browser setting permits the same. However, some parts of our site may not function optimally if the browser was to refuse cookies. In case cookies are disabled, then the most important function that you would lose is the ability to login to your account on Serendipity. Therefore, as a traveler, you would not be able to make a booking. As a guide or tour operator, you would not be able to respond to any client requests. Serendipity uses cookies for the following purposes: To help us recognize the particular browser as a previous visitor. We may also retain the user’s registration details on the cookie. To help us customize the content and advertisements provided on our websites and on other websites across the Internet. To help research the effectiveness of our website features, advertisements, and e-mail communications. We may also use technology called web beacons, which are similar to cookies, and are used to measure the effectiveness of email campaigns to determine which emails have been viewed and acted upon. It is also used to understand if you have reached our website after clicking on an advertisement hosted elsewhere. We also may use certain third party services for analytics, customer service, web marketing, fraud prevention, payment processing and other similar activities. These companies may make use of cookies or other tracking software. We do not have access to or control over cookies or any other technologies these third parties may use to collect such information. The privacy policies followed by these third parties are not covered by this Privacy Policy.
